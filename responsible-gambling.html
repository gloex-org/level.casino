Gambling should always be a form of entertainment. However, for some, it can spiral into a serious concern that affects personal finances, relationships, and health. At Level.casino, we are committed to providing you with the tools and information required to maintain control and play safely.

Self-Assessment: Is Your Gambling Still a Game?

If you answer "Yes" to more than two of these questions, we recommend seeking professional support:

Do you gamble to escape from a boring or unhappy life?
When gambling and you run out of money, do you feel lost and in despair and need to gamble again as soon as possible?
Do you gamble until your last penny is gone, even the fare home or the cost of a cup of tea?
Have you ever lied to cover up the amount of money or time you have spent gambling?
Have others ever criticized your gambling?

7 Tips for Safer Gambling

To ensure your experience remains positive, follow these industry-standard practices:

  • Don't think of gambling as a way to make money: The house always has an advantage in the long run.
  • Only gamble with money you can afford to lose: Never use money for rent, bills, or food.
  • Set a money limit in advance: Decide how much you can afford to lose before you start playing.
  • Set a time limit: It’s easy to lose track of time when playing. Set a timer.
  • Never chase your losses: Chasing losses usually leads to bigger losses.
  • Don't gamble when you're depressed or upset: Decision-making is harder when you're under emotional stress.
  • Balance gambling with other activities: Don't let gambling become your only form of leisure.

Player Protection Tools

Most reputable operators ranked on Level.casino offer technical tools to help you stay safe. We prioritize ranking casinos that provide:

  • Deposit Limits: Restrict the amount you can deposit over a daily, weekly, or monthly period.
  • Loss Limits: Limit the amount of real money you can lose from the deposits you make.
  • Session Limits: A "reality check" that notifies you when you've reached a certain time limit.
  • Self-Exclusion: A formal process where the operator closes your account for a period ranging from 6 months to 5 years.
